ICS Password Overview

ICS Single Sign-on

ICS uses single sign-on for all ICS services including Windows login, Linux shell accounts, and ICS email services. This means you only need to remember one set of credentials to access most ICS resources.

Your ICS account credentials are distinct and different from your UCInetID credentials. Some ICS applications require UCInetID credentials and will clearly indicate when to use them for authentication.
Quick Tip: Use our 24/7 chatbot in the bottom right corner of this page for AI guided answers from our wiki!

Reset Your ICS Password

Password Reset

How to reset:

  • Reset using your Security Question or Recovery Email address
  • Select your preferred method from the top navigation bar
  • Your ICS username is the same as your UCInetID
  • Review and follow the password requirements
  • For additional help, email helpdesk@ics.uci.edu with your information, problem description, and relevant screenshots

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is a security method that requires users to provide multiple forms of verification before gaining access.

The university uses an MFA solution called DUO.

MFA Requirements:

  • UCI Resources: DUO MFA is required for many UCI resources
  • ICS Resources: DUO MFA is required for sensitive ICS resources such as outward-facing servers like emp.ics.uci.edu or Openlab Jupyterhub

SSH Keys

ICS server users are strongly recommended to set up SSH keys to access ICS computing resources. SSH keys are required to connect to some ICS servers from off-campus when not using VPN.

Learn more: SSH Keys

Change or Reset Your ICS Password

You may reset your ICS password at ICS Password Page (password.ics.uci.edu) if you:

  • Know your current password, OR
  • Have set up and can answer your security question, OR
  • Have access to your alternate email address, which will be used to send a password reset message

You may send email to helpdesk@ics.uci.edu for help recovering your alternate email address.

When visiting the password site to reset your password, please note the links along the top bar:

Click on either Question or Email to reset a forgotten password.

To reset by email

You can reset your password by going to: https://password.ics.uci.edu

If you are off campus, including campus housing, you will need to connect to the campus VPN first.

-Enter your username: (should be the same as your UCInetID) -Enter your recovery address: (this was set when you created your account)

Our system will send your recovery address an email with a password reset link.

Contact helpdesk@ics.uci.edu if you need help determining your recovery address.

Types of Passwords Reset

The ICS Password Toolbox will change the following passwords:

  • ICS LDAP
  • ICS Google Workspace (for non-undergraduate accounts)
  • ICS AD
  • ICS Kerberos

Please contact Helpdesk via email (helpdesk@ics.uci.edu) if you need assistance with this process. Attach any relevant screenshots if you encounter error messages.

Password Management Tools

Password management tools aid in securely storing and conveniently recalling passwords. See a list of password/secret managers here:

  • Password/Secret Management

There are additional password managers available, but these are the ones that Computing Support has used in the past and can recommend:

  • KeePass Password Safe
    • Locally-managed encrypted password file
    • The KeePass program is distributed under the GNU GPLv2
  • 1Password
    • Cloud-based
    • Convenient browser plugins available
    • Convenient family options at no charge

Choosing a Password

  • Create a password using a combination of lowercase, uppercase, symbol, and number characters
  • Use a longer password (at least 8 characters in length)
  • Use a “passphrase” so that your password is easy to remember, for example YoUc@nnOts3e! for “You cannot see!”

How Often to Change Passwords

ICS Computing Support will require that you change your password under the following circumstances:

  • A significant change to the way that passwords are stored is implemented
  • There is reasonable suspicion that your account has been compromised

It is also a good idea to change your password after certain foreign travel, any use of unsecured networks, and any time that your computer becomes infected with viruses/malware.

Troubleshooting

Password was refused by the LDAP directory

If you receive the message “Password was refused by the LDAP directory”, this typically means that the server running the Password Reset page doesn't have permission to make changes to the LDAP database. Send email to helpdesk@ics.uci.edu.
