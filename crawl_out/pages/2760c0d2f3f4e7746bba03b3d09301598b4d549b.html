ICS Data Center

ICS Data Center Overview

The ICS Computing Support Team manages an on-premises data center. The data center is approximately 1600 square feet with 42 cabinets.

All ICS faculty and researchers are welcome to colocate equipment in the ICS Data Center, provided the equipment meets the requirements on this page.

The ICS Computing Support group can recommend configuration specifications, help obtain quotes, and in most cases manage equipment that is colocated in our space.

Space and power are currently constrained, so it is important that you consult with the ICS Computing Support group before ordering equipment.

Hardware Requirements

  • Rack Mountable: Equipment must mount neatly and securely into a standard 19“ cabinet. A rack mount kit is required.
  • Cabinets: Cabinets must fit through 83” tall door frames and elevator.
  • Advanced Out-of-Band Management: Often called BMC, iDRAC, IPMI, ILOM, or OOB, out-of-band management must provide complete remote administration with full console access.
  • Trusted Platform Module (TPM): Required for encrypting Linux installations. The TPM allows the passphrase to be stored securely for automatic boot processes.
  • Proper Airflow: The ICS Data Center uses hot and cold aisle separation. Equipment must intake cold air from the cold aisle and exhaust hot air into the hot aisle. This typically requires front-to-back airflow.
  • Proper Form Factor: Equipment must fit in a standard rack or a specialty rack must be provided. Equipment depth must be 30“ or less.
    • Exception: Servers with a depth of 31.5” may work if placed in racks where PDUs are not adjacent to each other on the same side.
  • IPv4 Compatibility: Equipment must support IPv4 addressing. IPv6 addresses are not currently available.
  • Power Supply Cables: The standard power connection is C13-C14. Limited C19-C20 connections are available in the data center.
    • Consider asking vendors if a C19-C14 cable is viable for powering the server (ideally 4ft length).

Requirements for ICS Managed Systems

ICS Operating System Support - These are the operating system support models offered for managed equipment.

ICS Computing support can manage equipment that comes assembled, is certified for the intended OS, and includes a minimal warranty. ICS researchers are welcome to house any system that meets the basic requirements and self-support but we are unable to provide support for self assembled or DIY builds.

Provided

The following resources are provided by ICS free of charge for all equipment in the ICS data center:

  • Up to 2 120V or 240V power connections per host.
  • Up to 2 1Gb/s per node plus 1 OOB connections.
  • Space in cabinet limited by availability and heat restrictions.

The following lists out additional recommendations for servers in the ICS Datacenter:

  • Redundant PSU
  • 10Gb/s Network

Equipment Purchase Workflow

  1. ICS Support can help get server quotes after being provided system specifications.
    • If the client gets server quotes without ICS Support's assistance, sufficient time must be given for review (minimum of 1 week).
  2. Servers are reviewed for:
    • Chassis depth and height.
    • Maximum power usage (applies especially to GPU servers)
    • Power input cord (C13 or C19). We have been surprised with C19 which we have fewer outlets for.
    • Network requirements (10GB, 1GB, fiber, etc)
    • Minimum 3 years warranty, with 5 years preferred.
  3. A helpdesk ticket should be opened with the quotes included for customer/ICS review and approval.

ICS Purchase and Reimbursement System - Use this system to submit purchases to the ICS business office.

Installation Workflow

  1. Create IP address with name chosen for the server
  2. Rack server and install.
  3. Test max power consumption of server.
    • Verify there is enough power capacity for the server
    • Label power usage on the server and in DCIM
  4. Re-rack if necessary or add more power to rack if available.

Property Tags

We can look up purchase orders in KFS to determine what equipment a property tag is meant for:

  1. Login to https://portal.uci.edu
  2. Click on “Finances/KFS” menu link and then the KFS Homepage under the “Tools & Support” section.
  3. Click on “Custom Document Search” and click on “Search - Purchase Orders”.
  4. Click on the heart icon to make it a favorite so you can see the it from the Home link.
  5. For the search, just enter the purchase order number in the “Purchase Order #” field.
  6. To find the original quote used for the purchase, look under “View Related Documents”.
    • Click on the link next to “REQUISITION”
    • That should take you to a new document with the quote under “Notes and Attachments” section.

Find Purchase Order No. Using Property Tag No.

  1. See Steps 1 and 2 above for logging into KFS under Property Tags section.
  2. Click on “Capital Assets” in the Sidebar and then “Asset Payment”.
  3. Put in the Property No. in the “Tag Number” field and click on Search.
  4. Scroll down to the bottom to see the result of the search (if it doesn't automatically scroll) and click on the link under “Asset Number” column.
  5. Expand the Payments section and click on “Hide” button. Then look for “Purchase Order Number”.
