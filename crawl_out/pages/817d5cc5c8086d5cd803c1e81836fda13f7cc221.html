Google Cloud Platform (GCP)

Summary

Google Cloud Platform is a set of modular cloud-based services that allow you to create anything from simple websites to complex applications. Cloud Platform provides the building blocks so you can quickly develop everything from simple websites to complex applications.

Google Cloud Platform is available to faculty, researchers and grad students via your ICS Google Apps Account.

Each ICS Google Apps account comes with a 60 day or $300 trial. You will need to provide a valid credit card in order to sign up.

If you would like to begin using the Google Cloud Platform please send email to helpdesk@ics.uci.edu.

GCP/AWS Project Management

Computing support can step into an active or passive role in managing GCP or AWS assets. Anything done in the ICS data center can likewise be done in the cloud including assignment of ics.uci.edu domain names.

  • Single sign on authentication

Google Opportunities

Apply for GCP for EDU Grants

Apply for GCP for EDU Grants Follow the hyperlink to apply for the GCP for EDU credit program. Once you apply, let helpdesk know so that we can help expedite the approval process. After you receive the grants, be sure to join the Google+ community of professors who have also received grants as they often share ideas about curriculum and post questions. If you have any questions about the program, be sure to post in the community contact CloudEDUGrants@google.com to get in touch with program managers.

NSF Big Data Grant

NSF Big Data Grant As of 2/17/2017, Google has resources that can help when applying for the NSF Big Data Grant. Send a note to helpdesk and we'll help you with the Google technical point of contact so he can address any questions you might have as you prepare your grant application.

Southern California GCP Fundamentals Training: Google has confirmation that a free fundamentals course (a $250 value!) at the end of March. It will likely be in their Venice Beach office. Send mail to helpdesk@ics.uci.edu if you would like more information.

Bill Pay Managers

Invoices

Bill pay managers can retrieve invoices after logging to the https://payments.google.com to retrieve invoices.

  • Choose the Subscriptions and Services tab at the top of the page.
  • Click on “mange” at the bottom of the card for the appropriate subscription
  • On the next page, in the Transactions box, click on the down arrow icon to download the transaction information.
Mail Settings

Bill pay managers may change their email settings by clicking on *Settings* at the top of this page https://payments.google.com

  1. Scroll down the page and click on *Mange Payment Users*
  2. Select your name
  3. In the box that unfurls, click on the information icon to read about the options.
  4. Click on the pencil icon to change your selection.

Full Billing Site

Advanced billing managers may also retrieve documents along with additional information such as usage and cost tables here:

https://console.cloud.google.com/billing

Choose the appropriate organization (typically ics.uci.edu) and then choose your project. ICS computing support uses “Computing Support Billing Account.” Chen LI uses “chenli-research” and Ramesh Jain uses “Ramesh Jain Billing Account”.

After choosing your project, statements will be available on the left hand menu under “Documents”. Receipts are available on the left hand menu under “Transactions''.

Send mail to helpdesk@ics.uci.edu if you feel you need access or are having trouble identifying which project to use.

Troubleshooting

Adding an APP to a Project

Q. Missing Required Permission

User received message that they lacked required permission consumerprocurement.orders.place on their billing account.

Fix

Assign consumerprocurement.orders.place to the billing account:https://cloud.google.com/marketplace/docs/access-control#billing-account

Use the glcoud command 

gcloud beta billing accounts  get-iam-policy 0116C6-256853-AD1005

Push the new roles up: 

gcloud beta billing accounts  set-iam-policy 0116C6-256853-AD1005  roles.yaml

roles.yaml: 

bindings:
- members:
  - user:dutran@ics.uci.edu
  - user:hans@ics.uci.edu
