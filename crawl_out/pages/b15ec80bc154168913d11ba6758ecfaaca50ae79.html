Computational Geometry Notes

Please find below slides and notes for the courses, CS 164 and CS 266, Computational Geometry.

Notes:

  • Week 0 and 1: Introduction and Convex hulls. Reading: Chapter 1 and the following:
    • Algorithm Analysis (Powerpoint)
    • Introduction (Powerpoint)
    • Convex hulls (Powerpoint)
    • QuickHull Algorithms (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on Big-Oh notation
    • Wikipedia article on convex hull algorithms.
    • Notes on randomized Quickhull.
    • Dynamic convex hulls. Reading: Section 1-3 in the following paper:
      • Overmars and van Leeuwen, Maintenance of Configurations in the Plane.
    • Output-sensitive convex hulls, mostly for CS 266 students: Chan's Output-Sensitive Convex Hull Algorithm (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on Chan's Convex Hull algoriothm.
  • Week 2: Line segment intersection. Reading: Chapter 2 and the following:
    • Wikipedia article on line-line intersections
    • Wikipedia article on the Bentley-Ottmann plane-sweep algorithm
    • Wikipedia article on WAVL Trees (a type of balanced binary search tree)
    • Zip trees (another type of (randomized) binary search tree)
    • Plane-sweep line segment intersection algorithm (Powerpoint)
    • Doubly-Connected Edge List, or DCEL (Annotated slides)
    • Overlaying planar subdivisions (Powerpoint)
  • Week 3: Polygon triangulation and lower envelopes. Reading: Chapter 3 and the following:
    • Polygon triangulation ( Powerpoint) (Annotated slides)
    • Wikipedia article on polygon triangulation
    • Lower Envelopes (Powerpoint) [This subject is not in the textbook.]
    • Wikipedia article on Davenport-Schinzel sequences
  • Week 4: Linear programming. Reading: Chapter 4 and the following:
    • Linear Programming ( Powerpoint)
    • d-Dimensional Linear Programming ( Powerpoint)
  • Week 5: Range searching. Reading: Chapter 5 and the following:
    • Range searching (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on range searching
    • Wikipedia article on range trees
    • Wikipedia article on fractional cascading
    • K-D trees (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on K-D trees
  • Week 6: Point location. Reading: Section 6.1 (the rest of Chapter 6 is optional) and the following:
    • Planar point location (Powerpoint) [Most of this is not in the textbook]
    • Wikipedia article on point location
    • For all students, read: Planar point location using persistent search trees, by Sarnak and Tarjan
    • Wikipedia article on persistent data structures
    • Kirkpatrick's planar point location method (Powerpoint) [This subject is not in the textbook]
    • For all students, read: Optimal search in planar subdivisions, by Kirkpatrick
  • Week 7: Voronoi diagrams. Reading: Chapter 7 and the following:
    • Voronoi diagrams and Their Applications (Powerpoint)
    • Constructing Voronoi diagrams (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on Voronoi diagrams
  • Week 8: Arrangements and duality. Reading: Chapter 8 and the following:
    • Point-line duality (Powerpoint)
    • Line arrangements (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on line arrangements
  • Week 9: Delaunay triangulations and 3D convex hulls. Reading: Chapter 9, Section 11.1, and the following:
    • Delaunay triangulations (Powerpoint) [most of this is not in the textbook]
    • Wikipedia article on Delaunay triangualtions
    • 3D Convex Hulls [these algorithms are not in the textbook]
  • Week 10: Windowing data structures. Reading: Chapter 10 and the following:
    • Windowing data structures (Powerpoint)
    • Wikipedia article on segment trees
    • Wikipedia article on interval trees
